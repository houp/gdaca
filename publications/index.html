Cellular automata can really solve the parity problem

Barbara Wolnik | Anna Nenca | Pedro Balbi | Bernard Baets
arXiv e-prints, 2025 Journal
DOI BibTeX
@article{Wolnik2025b,
    author = "Wolnik, Barbara and Nenca, Anna and Balbi, Pedro Paulo and Baets, Bernard De",
    title = "Cellular automata can really solve the parity problem",
    journal = "arXiv e-prints",
    volume = "arXiv:2501.08684",
    year = "2025",
    doi = "10.48550/arXiv.2501.08684",
    abstract = "Determining the parity (even vs. odd count of 1s) of a binary sequence by local processing has been a longstanding challenge in automata networks. The classical formulation asks for a one-dimensional cellular automaton that, for any odd-length cyclic binary configuration, converges to all-0 if the number of 1s is even or to all-1 if the number of 1s is odd:contentReference[oaicite:42]{index=42}. The only known solution (“BFO rule”) was later found to fail on certain configurations. In this paper, we present a corrected single-rule solution to the parity problem along with a complete proof of its correctness:contentReference[oaicite:43]{index=43}. Our result demonstrates that cellular automata *can* reliably solve the global parity task, resolving an open problem by providing a fully validated rule that achieves consensus on parity."
